Why should you visit Bátaszék?

Bátaszék is an important railway junction in Southern Transdanubia, where the lines from Pécs to Baja and from Szekszárd to Villány meet – making it a great starting point for exploring the countryside and local wine regions...

Church of the Assumption

The Church of the Assumption of the Roman Catholic Parish in Bátaszék is the city's symbol, with its sky-reaching tower topped by a 30-meter-high spire.

St. Urban’s Chapel

After the plague epidemic of 1739, the faithful of Bátaszék decided to build a chapel. In 1754, they constructed it on the vineyard hill in honor of St. Urban, the patron saint of vineyards. The chapel is a baroque-style building with a barrel vault and a small tower.

Get Directions
Downy Oak – Tree of the Year

Located right next to St. Urban’s Chapel, this downy oak won Hungary’s “Tree of the Year” title in 2015. A year later, it also won the “European Tree of the Year” award in 2016. The tree is estimated to be 300–400 years old and is the oldest tree in Bátaszék.
