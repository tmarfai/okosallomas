Százhalombattha railway station is located in Pesht County, in the town of Százhalombattha, and is operated by the Hungarian State Railways. The history of the station is closely intertwined with the development of the town, local industry, and the evolution of railway transport. During the 20th century, Százhalombattha grew into an industrial city, most notably with the establishment of a major oil refinery. This industrial growth also increased the importance of the railway station, as both freight transport and commuter traffic became essential to the town’s daily life.
