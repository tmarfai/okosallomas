Other nearby destinations

Vilma House

Built in 1839, the Vilma House is the first brick farmhouse of Somogyszob. It has been a listed monument since 1982, a museum exhibition site since 2009, and is also included on the World Heritage tentative list as part of a series of traditional house museums.

Kaszó Wildlife Park

The Kaszó Wildlife Park covers approximately 2.5 hectares and showcases all the major big game species (red deer, fallow deer, roe deer, wild boar) of the Kaszó hunting area in a natural environment.

Narrow-Gauge Railway

The railway line runs entirely through forest, passing through a beautiful landscape. With its diverse forest types, the Baláta Lake nature reserve, and the area's rich wildlife, the journey offers an unforgettable experience for visitors. The current line (Kaszó–Szenta) is 8 km long, with a travel time of 40 minutes.
