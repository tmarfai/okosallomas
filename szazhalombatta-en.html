Other Destinations Nearby

Archaeological Park

Hungary’s first open-air prehistoric museum opened its doors in 1996 with the aim of offering a glimpse into everyday life during the Bronze and Iron Ages. A key mission of the park is also to preserve and present the burial mounds that have retained Iron Age funeral customs to this day.

Saint Stephen Square

The main square of the city is one of Százhalombatta’s landmarks. With its fountains, flowerbeds, public sculptures, carillon, and art installations, it provides a special experience for both residents and visitors. This Mediterranean-style square is a true community space, where something exciting is always happening!

Matricum Museum

The museum is named after "Matrica" (pronounced: mátrika), a Roman-era settlement once located in the area of today’s Százhalombatta. The museum is housed in a former manor and serves as a local history museum, with exhibits based on artifacts found in the region. Its permanent exhibition guides visitors through history in one undivided space—from the Ice Age to the 19th century.
